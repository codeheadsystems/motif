# FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

## Customer FAQs

### What exactly is Motif, and how is it different from other productivity apps?

Motif is an event-tracking platform that works backward from what you actually do, rather than forward from what you plan to do. Traditional productivity tools—task managers, project planners, habit trackers—require you to predict and structure your work upfront. Motif takes the opposite approach: you log completed activities as they happen, and Motif discovers the patterns in your behavior.

The key difference is **learning vs. planning**. Other apps say "tell me what you'll do"; Motif says "tell me what you did, and I'll show you the patterns." This works because real life rarely follows predetermined plans. By analyzing completed events, Motif reveals recurring sequences, optimal timing, and workflow patterns that emerge organically from your actual behavior—not your aspirational plans.

For businesses, this difference is transformative. Instead of documenting theoretical processes, teams log real work as it happens. The organization's actual best practices emerge from the data and become reusable workflows.

### Why would I use Motif instead of just keeping a notes app or spreadsheet?

Three reasons: pattern discovery, workflow reusability, and progressive value.

**Pattern Discovery**: A notes app is write-only. You record information, but the app doesn't tell you anything new. Motif analyzes your events and surfaces insights you wouldn't spot manually: "You complete creative work best on Tuesday mornings" or "Successful projects follow this sequence." The intelligence layer transforms raw data into actionable knowledge.

**Workflow Reusability**: Once Motif discovers a pattern—say, how you successfully onboard clients—you can save it as a workflow template. When the next client arrives, you don't start from scratch or search old notes. The workflow is ready to deploy, refine, and improve. This compounds over time: every project makes you more systematic.

**Progressive Value**: Motif starts simpler than a spreadsheet (just tap to log an event) but grows more powerful than one (cloud sync, team collaboration, pattern AI). You get immediate utility without complexity, then unlock more sophisticated features only when you need them.

### How does pattern discovery actually work? What kinds of patterns will it find?

Motif's pattern engine analyzes temporal sequences, frequencies, and contexts in your logged events. It looks for:

**Temporal Patterns**: "You water plants every 7 days" or "Home maintenance clusters in March-April and September-October"

**Sequential Patterns**: "When you log 'client kickoff call,' it's typically followed by 'draft proposal' within 3 days, then 'proposal review' 2 days later"

**Contextual Patterns**: "Saturday morning events are usually health/exercise related" or "You batch creative work on Tuesdays and Thursdays"

**Project Patterns**: "Your completed renovation projects share this sequence: planning (2 weeks), material ordering (1 week), demolition (3 days), construction (variable), finishing (1 week)"

The AI doesn't just find repeated individual events—it discovers multi-step sequences that constitute workflows. When you've completed a type of project multiple times, Motif can say "here's the pattern that worked" and offer to save it as a reusable workflow.

### What happens to my data? Who owns it? Can you see it?

**You own your data. Period.**

Free tier: Your data never leaves your device. It's stored in local SQLite. We literally cannot see it because it never reaches our servers.

Premium/Business tiers: Your data is encrypted in transit and at rest on our servers. We can't read it, and we never share it with third parties—no analytics companies, no advertisers, nobody. The encryption keys are derived from your credentials.

At any time, you can export your complete data in standard formats (JSON, CSV). If you delete your account, we permanently delete all your data within 30 days. We're GDPR and CCPA compliant from day one because we believe user data ownership isn't negotiable.

### How does Motif handle privacy for business accounts? Can my boss see everything I log?

Business accounts have clear visibility boundaries designed to balance collaboration and privacy.

**What team members can see**:
- Events logged to shared projects
- Workflows attached to shared projects
- Team-level patterns (aggregated, not individual)

**What team members cannot see**:
- Events logged to your personal projects
- Your personal patterns and workflows
- Individual productivity metrics (unless you explicitly share them)

As a business administrator, you control project visibility. You can create private projects (visible only to assigned members), team projects (visible to specific groups), or company-wide projects. Team members always know which projects are shared and which are private.

The goal is organizational learning, not surveillance. Motif helps teams systematize what works, not monitor every individual action.

### What if I don't want to log events manually every time? Isn't that tedious?

We've optimized for speed. Logging an event in Motif takes about 3 seconds: open app, tap the event type or use the quick-add button, done. Timestamp is automatic, categories are optional, notes are optional. The barrier is as low as we can make it.

That said, we're exploring integrations and automation for future releases:
- Calendar integration: automatically create events from completed calendar items
- Email integration: log events when you send specific emails
- API access: connect to other tools you use
- Voice logging: speak the event instead of typing

But here's the philosophical point: manual logging has value. The act of consciously recording what you've completed creates awareness. It's a moment to reflect: "I just finished this; what should I do next?" That mindfulness is part of Motif's benefit, not a bug.

For high-volume scenarios (like a business logging every customer interaction), we'll prioritize automation. But for personal use, the few seconds of manual logging often provide unexpected clarity.

### How do workflows actually work? Can you give me a concrete example?

Let's walk through a real example: content creation.

**Discovery Phase** (Free or Premium tier):
You've been logging events for your blog over three months:
- "Research topic"
- "Draft outline"
- "Write first draft"
- "Edit and revise"
- "Create graphics"
- "Schedule publication"
- "Promote on social media"

After you complete your third blog post, Motif notices a pattern: successful posts follow this sequence, with specific timing (research: 1-2 days, drafting: 2-3 days, editing: 1 day, graphics: 1 day, promotion: ongoing).

**Workflow Creation** (Premium tier):
Motif prompts: "I've detected a pattern in your content creation. Would you like to save this as a workflow?" You review the suggested steps, adjust timing estimates based on the averages Motif calculated, and save it as "Blog Post Workflow."

**Workflow Application** (Premium tier):
Next time you start a blog post, you attach the "Blog Post Workflow" to a new project called "Q2 SEO Content Series." Motif now knows the expected sequence and timing. As you log events, it shows you where you are in the workflow and what typically comes next.

**Workflow Sharing** (Business tier):
You add a junior writer to your team. They can see the "Blog Post Workflow" you've refined. Instead of guessing the process or waiting for you to explain it, they follow the proven sequence. When they log events, Motif tracks their progress and can highlight deviations: "This post is taking longer at the editing stage than usual—need help?"

**Workflow Evolution** (Business tier):
After your junior writer completes five posts, they discover they work better by creating graphics first, which helps them visualize the content. They suggest updating the workflow. You review the data—their posts with graphics-first do complete faster—and update the organizational workflow. Now everyone benefits from the improvement.

This is how Motif transforms individual patterns into organizational knowledge.
